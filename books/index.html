Vicariously

In near-future California, jobs are scarce and unemployed masses spend their days in government housing: coffin-sized pods where tech lets them experience government-produced reality shows in five senses. New grad Beck Fiore wants to live life for herself, though—ad-free and in the flesh—and keep her underemployed ski pro dad out of the pods at any cost.

Beck thinks her dream is shot when she blows her only job prospect and discovers her dad is about to lose his house. When an old family friend gets Beckher the chance to try out for a snowboarding reality show, she jumps at it—even though auditioning requires expensive tech upgrades that will leave her penniless in a pod if she fails. She’ll have to build a huge following to have a shot at landing the gig, and on-screen romances are the easiest way to do that., Butso when she reconnects with childhood friend and rival snowboarder Ash at the tryouts, she can’t tell if their chemistry is real or all for the audience—a question that only grows when they start fake-dating at the suggestion of their agent.

The closer Beck gets to a contract, though, the more she questions how real her new life will be and whether having money to save herself and her father is worth being part of the system she’s saving them from.
